At SRI SAI ITI, we offer a range of specialized courses designed to equip students with the skills needed in today's competitive job market. Here are some of the courses we provide:

    Electrician (NSQF)

    The Electrician trade in ITI (Industrial Training Institute) focuses on training individuals in the fundamental skills required to work as electricians in various industries. This vocational course teaches students about the installation, maintenance, and repair of electrical systems, wiring, circuits, and equipment.

    Key areas of training include:

    • Electrical Wiring: Learning about residential, commercial, and industrial wiring systems, including how to connect circuits and ensure safety.
    • Electrical Equipment: Understanding the operation and repair of electrical equipment such as motors, transformers, and switchboards.
    • Safety Standards: Emphasizing safety protocols, wiring codes, and electrical hazard prevention.
    • Tools and Techniques: Mastering tools like pliers, wire cutters, and multimeters, along with techniques for fault diagnosis and repair.
    • Theory and Practical Application: A balance of theoretical knowledge and hands-on training to develop practical skills for real-world electrical work.

    This trade prepares students for careers in sectors such as construction, manufacturing, maintenance, and power plants, enabling them to work as electricians, electrical technicians, or in related roles.

    Electronics Mechanic (NSQF)

    The Electronics trade in ITI focuses on training individuals in the installation, maintenance, and repair of electronic equipment and systems. Students gain practical knowledge and skills to work with various types of electronics used in everyday life and industries.

    Key areas of training include:

    • Basic Electronics: Understanding circuits, components like resistors, capacitors, transistors, and how they function in electronic devices.
    • Consumer Electronics: Repair and servicing of household electronic appliances such as televisions, radios, and refrigerators.
    • Electronic Testing: Learning how to use tools like oscilloscopes, multimeters, and signal generators for testing and troubleshooting.
    • Microelectronics: Basics of semiconductors, microcontrollers, and integrated circuits.
    • Soldering and Wiring: Techniques for assembling and soldering electronic components onto circuit boards.

    This trade prepares students to work in various fields, including consumer electronics, telecommunications, automation, and industrial electronics, making them eligible for roles like electronics technician, maintenance engineer, or service engineer.

    Diesel Mechanic (NSQF)

    The Diesel Mechanic trade in ITI focuses on training individuals in the repair, maintenance, and servicing of diesel engines used in vehicles, machines, and other heavy equipment. The course teaches students to work on various diesel-powered systems used in transport, agriculture, and industries.

    Key areas of training include:

    • Diesel Engine Operation: Learning how diesel engines work, their components (e.g., fuel injection system, cylinder head), and understanding their power generation and performance.
    • Maintenance and Repair: Hands-on experience in diagnosing and repairing engine faults, performing regular maintenance tasks like oil changes and air filter replacements.
    • Engine Overhaul: Learning how to disassemble and reassemble engines, check for wear and tear, and replace faulty parts.
    • Fuel Systems: Understanding fuel injection systems, carburetors, and other related components critical to diesel engine performance.
    • Tools and Techniques: Familiarity with mechanical tools such as wrenches, torque meters, and diagnostic equipment.

    The Diesel Mechanic trade prepares students to work in sectors like transportation, construction, agriculture, and heavy machinery, enabling them to become diesel mechanics, service technicians, or engine specialists.
